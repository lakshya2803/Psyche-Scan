Mountain Pose

A standing pose that promotes strength and stability. It improves posture and increases awareness.

Benefits: Improves posture, strengthens thighs and ankles, reduces flat feet.

Child's Pose

Kneel on the mat, sit back on your heels, and extend your arms forward with your forehead resting on the floor.

Benefits: Relieves back and neck pain, reduces stress and fatigue.

Warrior Pose

Stand with one foot forward, knee bent, arms extended overhead (Warrior I) or to the sides (Warrior II), promoting strength and balance.

Benefits: Builds strength and stamina, improves balance.

Downward Dog

An inversion that stretches the whole body and builds strength.

Benefits: Strengthens arms and legs, improves digestion.

Corpse Pose

Lie on your back, arms by your sides, and close your eyes. Focus on deep, rhythmic breathing.

Benefits: Promotes relaxation, reduces anxiety, and improves overall well-being.

Bridge Pose

Lie on your back, bend your knees, and lift your hips toward the ceiling.

Benefits: Helps in reducing anxiety and stress.
